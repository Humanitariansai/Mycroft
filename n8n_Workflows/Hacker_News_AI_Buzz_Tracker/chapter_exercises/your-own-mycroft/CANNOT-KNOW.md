# What No Model and No Market Chatter Can Tell Me

This is charter lists the things the assistant cannot know and the things the noise around a stock cannot settle. Every audit on this desk is read against it.

## What no model can know about me

- My risk tolerance. As a beginner I should treat it as low until I have learned more. If I ever put real money in, the honest test is whether I could watch the position fall twenty or thirty percent without panic selling, and right now I do not know that I could.
- My time horizon. The thesis calls META a long term investment. Before any real decision I would need to set how many years that means for me and whether I can leave the money untouched for that long.
- My full financial picture. Whether I even have money I can afford to lose, my other obligations, and when I might need it.
- Whether I can defend a trade. If someone asked why I bought, I would need to point to a filing line and my own reasoning, not to an AI paragraph or a headline. 

## What market chatter cannot tell me

- Whether a price move is signal or noise. A green or red day, a viral post, or a loud opinion is not evidence about the business.
- What is already priced in. The things everyone is repeating are usually already reflected in the price.
- Whether a confident story is true. Fluent does not mean correct, whether the story comes from a pundit or from an AI.

## My sources of truth

- META's own filings, the 10-K and the 10-Q.
- The actual options chain if I ever look at signals.
- Not tweets, not forum posts, not a pundit's price target, not a smooth AI summary.

## The line I do not cross

The assistant gathers, structures, and flags. It does not decide and it does not place trades. Any real decision is mine, I cite the source, and I own the outcome. Until I have the knowledge to do that, this stays a learning exercise.
