# Chapter 2 Project: The Reallocation Principle (META)

Project: Your Own Mycroft. Ticker: META. Builds on the Chapter 1 desk in `../your-own-mycroft/`.

## Exercise 1

AI assistance is appropriate for these tasks this chapter:

- Inventorying my thesis into steps and sorting each as preparation or judgment. This works because it is mechanical decomposition of text I supply, and I can re-sort any line I disagree with.
- Sketching, from the claim audit, which open claims could plausibly flip the decision if they resolved against me. This works because it is structured reasoning over inputs I give it, and I confirm each could-flip against my own thesis.
- Drafting the time-cost side of the plan, roughly how long each piece of evidence would take to pull. This works because it is estimation and formatting that I sanity check against my own sense of the effort.

The tell is the same as before: I am using AI well when I can evaluate the output against independent criteria.

## Exercise 2

These tasks require my own judgment:

- Deciding which claim actually moves my decision. The weighting depends on my thesis, my risk tolerance, and my horizon, none of which the model knows, so it will rank by what sounds important in the text rather than by what changes my call.
- Deciding that the research is done and the position is ready. The model carries no accountability for capital and cannot feel when the evidence is thin, so a confident analysis complete is the recovered-hours-vanish failure this chapter warns about.
- Judging whether an options signal is even worth research time, or is just short dated retail noise. That triage needs the actual term structure and market context the model did not ground in.

The tell is that I have crossed the line when AI output becomes my reason to act rather than a tool for reaching my own decision.
