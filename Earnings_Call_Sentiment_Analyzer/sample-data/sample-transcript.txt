Northstar Cloud Systems, Inc.
Q3 Fiscal Year 2026 Earnings Conference Call

OPERATOR INTRODUCTION

Operator:
Good afternoon, and welcome to Northstar Cloud Systems' third quarter fiscal 2026 earnings call. All participants are currently in listen-only mode. After the prepared remarks, we will open the call for questions. This call is being recorded.

PREPARED REMARKS

Maya Chen - Chief Executive Officer:
Thank you, operator, and thank you everyone for joining us. We delivered a strong quarter, with durable demand across our data platform and security products. Revenue grew 18 percent year over year, ahead of the outlook we shared in May. Our largest customers expanded their workloads, and renewal rates remained above 95 percent.

The team also made meaningful progress on product reliability. Service availability improved to 99.99 percent, and customer support response times fell by 22 percent. We are pleased with these results, but we are not satisfied with every part of our execution. New customer additions were below our internal plan in Europe, where longer procurement cycles created friction late in the quarter.

FINANCIAL RESULTS

Daniel Ortiz - Chief Financial Officer:
Third quarter revenue was $842 million, an increase of 18 percent year over year. Subscription revenue was $764 million, while professional services revenue was $78 million. Gross margin was 74.6 percent, roughly flat from the prior year and 40 basis points above our guidance midpoint.

Operating expenses increased 15 percent as we continued to invest in research and development. Free cash flow was $176 million. Remaining performance obligations reached $3.4 billion, although current RPO growth moderated to 14 percent because several large renewals shifted into the fourth quarter.

GUIDANCE AND OUTLOOK

Daniel Ortiz - Chief Financial Officer:
For the fourth quarter, we expect revenue between $865 million and $875 million, representing approximately 15 percent growth at the midpoint. We expect non-GAAP operating margin of 21 to 22 percent. Foreign exchange is expected to be a two-point headwind to reported revenue growth.

Maya Chen - Chief Executive Officer:
Our pipeline is healthy and demand for the new security suite is encouraging. At the same time, we are planning conservatively because enterprise approval cycles remain unpredictable. We believe our platform strategy positions us well for durable long-term growth.

QUESTION AND ANSWER SESSION

Operator:
We will now begin the question-and-answer session. Our first question comes from Sarah Kim with Summit Securities.

Sarah Kim - Equity Research Analyst:
Congratulations on the revenue performance. Can you explain what drove the slower new customer additions in Europe, and whether that weakness continued into July?

Maya Chen - Chief Executive Officer:
The primary issue was timing rather than competitive loss. Several customers completed technical evaluations but could not finish procurement before quarter end. Activity improved in July, though it is still too early to call the issue fully resolved. We have added regional deal support and simplified the approval path for smaller deployments.

Sarah Kim - Equity Research Analyst:
That is helpful. How should investors think about the margin impact of those additional resources?

Daniel Ortiz - Chief Financial Officer:
The investment is included in our fourth quarter outlook. We expect a modest near-term cost, but it should not change the full-year margin framework.

Operator:
Our next question comes from Michael Torres with Harbor Capital.

Michael Torres - Senior Software Analyst:
Current RPO growth decelerated again this quarter. Is that entirely timing, or are customers reducing contract duration and seat commitments?

Daniel Ortiz - Chief Financial Officer:
Most of the deceleration reflects the renewal timing I mentioned. We did see some customers favor three-year contracts over five-year contracts, which creates a smaller initial RPO balance. Seat expansion remained positive, but the pace was lower than last year.

Michael Torres - Senior Software Analyst:
And on the security suite, are early wins coming from existing customers or net-new logos?

Maya Chen - Chief Executive Officer:
The majority are existing platform customers, which gives us an efficient route to market. We also signed 34 net-new security customers during the quarter. Win rates improved, customer feedback has been excellent, and the product roadmap is ahead of schedule.

Operator:
This concludes our question-and-answer session. I will now turn the call back to Maya Chen for closing remarks.

Maya Chen - Chief Executive Officer:
Thank you to our customers, partners, and employees. We have important work ahead, especially in Europe, but the fundamentals of the business are strong. We remain focused on disciplined execution and long-term customer value.
